## The Mission

Build a vertically integrated seafood supply chain in Central America (Nicaragua primary, Honduras secondary) that:
- Catches bait fish (herring, mackerel) and premium species at low cost
- Processes locally, exports directly to Canada and USA bait markets
- Produces fishmeal and fish oil for regional aquaculture feed supply
- Undercuts Peruvian/Chilean imported fishmeal (currently ~$1,992/tonne March 2026 — near historic high)
- Redistributes wealth into the local community — generational model, NOT a smash-and-grab

**This is a generational business. Not a 10-year exit. The community equity model is a competitive moat, not a charity.**

## Geography

**Primary: Nicaragua (Pacific side)**
- Base of operations: Estero Real / Corinto fishing port
- Shrimp aquaculture zone: Estero Real, near El Viejo
- Processing → Managua trucking hub
- Export: Puerto Corinto → Saint John NB / US East Coast

**Secondary: Honduras (Atlantic/Caribbean side)**
- Target species: Atlantic herring, Atlantic mackerel
- Processing hub candidate: San Lorenzo / Puerto Cortés
- Regulatory barriers exist (foreign captain prohibition, majority local ownership required) BUT David Arenburg has a relationship with the Honduran Ambassador — this is the workaround angle
- Honduras withdrew from ICSID (international arbitration) August 2024 — risk factor

## Supply Gap — Why Now

- **Atlantic mackerel**: Canadian commercial fishery closed since 2022. DFO moratorium ongoing. 500 tonne TAC for personal use only — cannot be sold.
- **US herring**: ACL cut 89% in a single year (2025).
- **Peruvian fishmeal**: Lower supply expected H1 2026 due to reduced quota. Prices at historic highs.
- **Nicaragua–China FTA**: Zero tariffs effective January 2024. Underutilized by most operators.
- **Central America fishmeal import dependency**: Region imports 100% of fishmeal from Peru/Chile. Freight disadvantage. Christopher can produce locally at fraction of cost.
